Nelson Mandela

The man who fought for his race

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  1. 1918 - July 18: Nelson Mandela was born in the small village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa's Cape Province.
  2. 1943 - Mandela joined the African National Congress (ANC), an organization that would later play a pivotal role in the struggle against apartheid.
  3. 1961 - The ANC adopted a more radical stance, and Mandela became involved with the armed wing of the ANC, Umkhonto we Sizwe (Spear of the Nation).
  4. 1962 - Mandela was arrested and subsequently imprisoned for his involvement in anti-apartheid activities.
  5. 1964 - Mandela and several other anti-apartheid leaders were sentenced to life in prison during the Rivonia Trial.
  6. 1990 - February 11: Nelson Mandela was released from prison, marking the end of his 27-year imprisonment.
  7. 1991 - Mandela was elected as the President of the ANC.
  8. 1993 - Mandela and South African President F.W. de Klerk were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts in dismantling apartheid.
  9. 1994 - April 27: South Africa held its first multiracial democratic elections, and Nelson Mandela was elected as the country's first black president.
  10. 1996 - Mandela signed a new democratic constitution into law, emphasizing equal rights for all South Africans.
  11. 1999 - Mandela's presidency came to an end after he served a single term.
  12. 2004 - Mandela announced his retirement from public life.
  13. 2013 - December 5: Nelson Mandela passed away at the age of 95 in Johannesburg, South Africa.
